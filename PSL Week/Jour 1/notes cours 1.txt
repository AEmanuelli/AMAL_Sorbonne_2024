7 étapes pour étudier un événement

Step 1 : definition of the event = (event window, date 0= event date)
Step 2 : Selection criteria : summary statistics and outliers 
Step 3 : Normal and abnormal returns : isolation of the movements event related is 2step : compute time series of abnormal return fro each firm 
        and average those abnormal returns over all the firms in the sample, to try to average out the noise 
        Abnormal return = observed - expected. C'est dans le expected qu'on utilise du ML
Step 4 : Estimation procedure 120 days to 250 days to estimate 
Step 5 : Test Procedure first, then test the null hyopothesis 
Step 6 : Results = additional tests : robustness ? incidence of outliers ? sensitivity to the choice of estimation window ? 
Step 7 : Interpretation and conclusion 

First models : 
    1. The constant mean retrun model returns mean + noise, suimplifying assumptions : mean of the noise = 0, homoscedaticity (the variance of the noise is alwayys the same)
            + pas d'autocorrélation, l'éspérance du produit du bruit à 2 time step différentes est nulle 
    2. The Market model : linear relationship between return of the stock and return of the marcket (proxied by an index)
                        R(i,t) = alpha(i) + beta(i)*R(m,t) + zeta(i,t)
                        zeta(i,t) modélise le bruit, on garde les mêmes hypothèses que pour le constant mean 

Other models : index model alpha = 0 beta = 1 ; fator models (3 ou 5) avec comme autres régresseurs les paramètres SMB et HML
